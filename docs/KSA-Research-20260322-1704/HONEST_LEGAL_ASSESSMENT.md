# Honest Legal Assessment — Is There a Real Case?

## The Short Answer

**Yes, but it's a contract case, not a fraud case.**

The strongest claim — breach of Article VIII (surplus return) — is real, provable with existing 990 data, and worth approximately $1M–$1.4M. The self-dealing structure is genuinely problematic and would likely result in court-ordered governance reforms.

The fraud allegations are aggressive lawyering. They might stick if discovery reveals smoking-gun emails, but on publicly available evidence alone, "fraud" is a stretch. What we actually have evidence of is:

- **A badly governed nonprofit** — not necessarily a corrupt one
- **A structural conflict of interest** — that may be incompetent rather than intentional
- **Contract non-compliance** — which is more likely laziness/inertia than theft
- **An expense spike** — that could easily be a legitimate capital project

## What's Actually Wrong vs. What's Alleged

### What's Actually Wrong (Provable)

1. **Article VIII is being violated.** Surpluses exist. They're not returned. This is a contract breach.
2. **The governance structure has an inherent conflict.** McCormick on both sides of the management contract is indefensible as a governance matter.
3. **No competitive bidding.** Industry standard requires it. KSA doesn't do it.
4. **No conflict of interest policy.** Basic governance. KSA doesn't have one.
5. **KSA is unresponsive.** BBB F rating. Refused arbitration. This is real.

### What's Alleged But Unproven

1. **"Multi-million dollar fraud"** — This is Mills Branch's characterization. The actual surplus retention over 14 years is ~$1M, not "multi-millions."
2. **McCormick is stealing money** — No evidence of this. She may be overpaid through KAM, but overpayment through a self-dealing structure isn't theft.
3. **The 2024 expense spike is suspicious** — It could easily be a roof replacement, a parking lot resurfacing, or deferred tree removal. We don't know.
4. **KAM overcharges** — We don't know what KAM charges. It might be at market rate.
5. **Towing kickbacks** — Zero evidence. Texas law prohibits them, and there's no indication KSA receives anything from EMC Towing.

## The Real Opportunity

The legal case is a **lever**, not the goal. The goal is governance reform. Here's why:

**Litigation burns the village down.** If RGDGC or allied villages sue KSA into insolvency, the parks suffer. River Grove Park's maintenance budget drops. The disc golf course deteriorates. Nobody wins.

**The constructive path is to offer a better alternative.** Instead of destroying KSA, replace what it does — with better technology, more transparency, community employment, and modern management.

This is where the RGDGC platform you're building becomes the weapon — not a lawsuit.
