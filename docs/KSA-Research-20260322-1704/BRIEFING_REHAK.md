## What We're Proposing

The **River Grove Parks Conservancy (RGPC)** — a 501(c)(3) that partners with KSA to bring modern park management to River Grove. Think of it as the East End Park trail-building model you pioneered, scaled across all of River Grove, powered by technology, and funded sustainably.

### What Makes This Different from 2019

In 2019, Scott and Coleman offered volunteer labor. We're offering a **complete operating system:**

## The Technology Platform

Our team has built a full-stack technology platform — FastAPI backend, React Native mobile app, PostgreSQL with PostGIS spatial database, Mapbox satellite imagery, and an AI chatbot. It's open source.

**What this means for park management:**

- **Maintenance requests:** Residents photo + GPS-tag an issue (downed tree, broken bench, trail washout). It appears in a work queue. Youth workers see it in their app, fix it, log completion with photos. The resident gets notified. Everything is tracked.

- **Park access:** Digital QR code replaces the 1976 K-sticker. Scan at entry. No more towing residents who forgot to update their windshield sticker.

- **Financial transparency:** Real-time dashboard shows every dollar: where revenue comes from, where expenses go, budget vs. actual. No more scanned PDF 990s. No more 14 years between published budgets.

- **Environmental monitoring:** Conservation Corps members log water levels, tree health, wildlife observations, and trail conditions — all GPS-tagged, all aggregated into a living database of River Grove's ecological health.

- **Flood preparedness:** IoT sensors at key drainage points feed data into the platform. When water levels rise, the app alerts park workers and visitors. We become an early warning node in the broader flood monitoring network.

## The KSA Context (You Know This Better Than We Do)

You've served on the KSA Parks Committee for over a decade. You know the constraints: volunteer board, zero employees, limited budget, aging infrastructure.

We're not attacking KSA. We're offering to take the operational burden off the volunteer board for one park — the park with the most active user community and the most flood risk.

### Our Approach

1. **Months 1-6:** Demonstrate. Volunteer maintenance, run events, deploy technology, hire first 10 youth workers. Document everything with the same rigor you bring to ReduceFlooding.com.

2. **Months 4-10:** Build coalition. Present to village boards, chamber, Flickinger, Super Neighborhood Council. Get your perspective and guidance.

3. **Month 8-14:** Present to KSA Board. Formal MOU proposal: RGPC manages River Grove under KSA oversight. Same cost. Modern technology. Youth jobs. Transparency.

We'd value your counsel on timing, framing, and the Parks Committee dynamics.
