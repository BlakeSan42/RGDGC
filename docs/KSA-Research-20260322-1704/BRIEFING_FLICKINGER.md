## 3-Minute Summary

We're a group of Kingwood residents who maintain River Grove Park's 21-hole disc golf course on a volunteer basis. We're proposing to formalize this as the **River Grove Parks Conservancy** — a 501(c)(3) that brings modern technology, financial transparency, youth employment, and community programming to River Grove Park.

**What we're asking from you:** Awareness and support. Not funding.

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **City cost** | $0 |
| **Youth jobs created** | 10 (Year 1) → 40+ (Year 3) |
| **Events/year** | 20+ (tournaments, cleanups, festivals) |
| **Technology** | Mobile app for park management, digital access, real-time budget dashboard |
| **Model** | Hermann Park Conservancy / Memorial Park Conservancy, adapted for suburban community |
| **Flood preparedness** | Conservation Corps installs IoT flood sensors at River Grove drainage points |

## Why This Matters for District E

### 1. Youth Employment — Kingwood Has a Gap

KSA manages 356 acres of parkland and employs **zero** people. Not one youth trail worker. Not one park ambassador. Not one summer intern. We're proposing to change that.

| Tier | Age | Rate | Source |
|------|-----|------|--------|
| Trail Crew (summer) | 16-18 | $15/hr | HIRE Houston Youth |
| Park Ambassadors | 18-22 | $17/hr | Assessment revenue + grants |
| Tech Apprentices | 18-24 | $18/hr | Software development on our platform |
| Conservation Corps | 18-25 | $2,560/mo | AmeriCorps / Texas Conservation Corps |
| Event Coordinators | 21+ | $20/hr | Event revenue |

We've modeled this across 1,000 Monte Carlo simulations. Median outcome: **60 unique youth employed over 5 years**, **$667K in wages paid**.

### 2. Fiscal Responsibility — Same Cost, 10x Services

KSA charges residents ~$41/unit/year. For that money they mow grass, maintain entryways, and contract with a management company. No events. No youth. No technology. No transparency.

We can deliver all of that **plus** 40 youth jobs, 20+ events, a mobile app, real-time budgets, and flood sensors — at the same per-unit cost.

### 3. Flood Preparedness — River Grove Is Ground Zero

River Grove Park was under **20 feet of water** during Harvey. Our Conservation Corps would install flood sensors at key drainage points, monitor watershed conditions, and maintain the Kingwood Diversion Ditch corridor through River Grove.

This aligns directly with your Lake Houston Dam Spillway and Diversion Ditch priorities. We'd be your eyes and ears on the ground at River Grove.

### 4. Community Self-Sufficiency — The Median Madness Model at Scale

Your Median Madness cleanup events are exactly the model we're scaling. Volunteers maintaining community infrastructure, organized through technology, building civic pride. We're just doing it year-round across 74 acres.

## What We've Built (Technology)

Our team has built a full technology platform — the same tools used by professional parks departments — open source, running on $6K/year in hosting:

- **Mobile app** (iOS + Android) — park status, event registration, digital check-in
- **Admin dashboard** — staff scheduling, budget tracking, maintenance work orders
- **Real-time budget transparency** — every dollar visible to every resident
- **AI chatbot** — 24/7 park info, rules, event details (no more phone calls to a management company)
- **GPS mapping** — satellite imagery, elevation data, trail maps
- **QR-code system** — digital park access, replacing 1976-era windshield stickers
