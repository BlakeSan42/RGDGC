### Engagement Scope

The forensic accountant will be engaged to:

1. **Trace funds** — Follow every dollar from homeowner assessment through KSA to final recipient
2. **Quantify damages** — Calculate retained surplus with prejudgment interest
3. **Identify anomalies** — Statistical analysis of expense patterns, especially 2024
4. **Evaluate self-dealing** — Quantify KAM's total revenue from the Kingwood ecosystem
5. **Benchmark management fees** — Compare KAM fees to competitive market rates
6. **Assess 990 accuracy** — Review related-party disclosures and expense classifications
7. **Opine on governance** — Whether financial controls are adequate for an organization of this size

## Analysis Framework

### Module 1: Surplus Retention Analysis

**Objective:** Prove that KSA systematically retained budget surpluses in violation of Article VIII.

**Methodology:**
1. Obtain KSA's internal fiscal year-end financial statements (2011–2024)
2. Calculate surplus/deficit for each year: Revenue − Expenses = Surplus
3. For each surplus year, determine:
   - Was a surplus calculation performed?
   - Was the surplus amount communicated to Member Associations?
   - Were any funds returned to Member Associations?
   - If not returned, what was the stated justification?
4. Calculate cumulative retained surplus with prejudgment interest at 5%/annum

**Expected Output:**

| Year | Revenue | Expenses | Surplus | Returned | Retained | Cumul. Retained | Interest |
|------|---------|----------|---------|----------|----------|----------------|----------|
| 20XX | $XXX | $XXX | $XXX | $0 / $XXX | $XXX | $XXX | $XXX |

### Module 2: KAM Revenue Tracing

**Objective:** Quantify total KAM revenue from the Kingwood ecosystem (KSA + villages).

**Methodology:**
1. Obtain KAM management contract(s) with KSA — determine fee structure and amounts
2. Obtain KAM management contracts with individual village HOAs
3. Trace KAM bank deposits from Kingwood-related sources
4. Calculate total KAM revenue attributable to Kingwood
5. Compare to market rates for similar services

**Expected Output:**

|--------|-----------|--------------|
| KSA management contract | $XXX | $XXX |
| Village HOA 1 contract | $XXX | $XXX |
| Village HOA 2 contract | $XXX | $XXX |
| ... | ... | ... |
| **Total KAM Revenue from Kingwood** | **$XXX** | **$XXX** |

### Module 3: Expense Anomaly Analysis

**Objective:** Identify and explain the 2024 expense spike and any other anomalies.

**Methodology:**
1. Obtain detailed expense records for 2019–2024 (check register, general ledger)
2. Categorize all expenses by type (parks, G&A, entryways, capital, legal, insurance)
3. Perform year-over-year variance analysis
4. Identify all new vendors in 2024
5. Flag any payments to related parties
6. Calculate statistical significance of 2024 spike (Z-score, standard deviation analysis)
7. Identify any expenses that appear personal rather than organizational

**Key Questions:**
- What specific expenditures caused the $556,486 increase?
- Were these expenditures budgeted and approved by the Board?
- Do any payments go to entities related to McCormick or Board members?
- Are legal fees for the Mills Branch lawsuit disclosed?

### Module 4: Related-Party Transaction Analysis

**Objective:** Identify all transactions between KSA and entities related to McCormick or Board members.

**Methodology:**
1. Obtain complete vendor payment history (2011–2024)
2. Cross-reference vendors against:
   - KAM
   - McCormick (personal, family, other businesses)
   - Board member names and addresses
   - Texas SOS entity search for related companies
3. Identify any vendor that shares an address with KSA/KAM
4. Review 990 Schedule L for related-party disclosures
5. Flag any undisclosed related-party transactions

### Module 5: Management Fee Benchmarking

**Objective:** Determine whether KAM's management fee is at, above, or below market rate.

**Methodology:**
1. Determine KAM's actual management fee from contract/records
2. Survey market rates for master association management in Houston metro:
   - Obtain quotes from 3+ management companies (FirstService, Associa, RealManage, Sterling ASI)
   - Adjust for KSA's specific scope (5 parks, 356 acres, 23,354 units)
3. Calculate excess fee (if any): KAM Fee − Market Average = Excess
4. Extrapolate excess over contract duration for damages

### Module 6: Form 990 Accuracy Review

**Objective:** Determine whether KSA's 990 filings accurately report all material information.

**Methodology:**
1. Obtain complete 990s with all schedules (2011–2024)
2. Review Part VII: Officer compensation
   - Should McCormick's KAM compensation be reported as a "key employee" or "highest compensated employee"?
3. Review Part VIII: Revenue
   - Should field lease payments be reported as rental income rather than program services?
4. Review Schedule L: Related-Party Transactions
   - Is the KAM contract disclosed?
   - Are any other related-party transactions disclosed?
5. Review Part IX: Statement of Functional Expenses
   - Do reported categories match actual expenditure patterns?

**IRS Reporting Requirements for 501(c)(4):**
- Part VII-B requires reporting the five highest compensated employees earning >$100K
- If KAM's fee exceeds $100K, and McCormick is the de facto executive, this may require reporting
- Schedule L requires disclosure of interested person transactions where the aggregate exceeds $100K

## Forensic Report Structure

### Section 1: Background and Engagement
- Engagement description and scope
- Materials reviewed
- Methodology

### Section 2: Findings — Surplus Retention
- Year-by-year analysis with supporting data
- Total damages with interest calculation

### Section 3: Findings — Self-Dealing and Management Fees
- KAM contract analysis
- Total KAM revenue from Kingwood ecosystem
- Market rate comparison
- Excess fee calculation

### Section 4: Findings — Financial Anomalies
- 2024 expense spike analysis
- Statistical significance testing
- Vendor analysis
- Related-party transactions

### Section 5: Findings — 990 Reporting
- Disclosure adequacy assessment
- Material omissions identified
- Regulatory compliance concerns

### Section 6: Damages Summary
- Direct damages (retained surplus + interest)
- Excess management fees
- Total quantified damages

### Section 7: Conclusions and Opinions
- Expert opinions on financial management adequacy
- Governance assessment
- Fraud risk indicators
